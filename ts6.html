Reputation

Sixth Studio Album • 2017

Electropop EDM 15 Songs

Taylor Swift's darker, edgier album exploring themes of media scrutiny, love, and reinvention after a public hiatus.

About the Album

Reputation is Taylor Swift's sixth studio album, released in 2017 after a three-year hiatus following intense media scrutiny and public controversies. The album marks a significant departure from her previous work, embracing a darker, edgier electropop sound with heavy EDM and trap influences. The album's themes center around Swift's reputation in the media, her response to public perception, and her discovery of a private love amid the chaos. Swift intentionally avoided all traditional media promotion for the album, instead using social media and mysterious marketing tactics to build anticipation.

The album features some of Swift's most defiant and experimental tracks, including "Look What You Made Me Do," "...Ready for It?," "Delicate," and "Getaway Car." "Look What You Made Me Do" served as the lead single and became a cultural phenomenon, with its music video breaking YouTube records. The song's lyrics directly addressed Swift's public feuds and media portrayal, while "Delicate" represented the album's softer side, exploring the vulnerability of new love. The production throughout the album is characterized by heavy synthesizers, distorted vocals, and industrial beats.

Reputation was a commercial success despite Swift's unconventional promotion strategy, debuting at number one on the Billboard 200 and selling 1.238 million copies in its first week. This made it the best-selling album of 2017 in the United States. While the album received mixed reviews from critics upon release, it has since been reappraised more favorably and developed a dedicated fan following. The Reputation Stadium Tour, which supported the album, became the highest-grossing North American tour in history at the time, grossing over $266 million.

Unlike her earlier albums, Swift has not yet released a "Taylor's Version" of Reputation, as it was the first album released under her new contract with Republic Records after leaving Big Machine. However, fans anticipate its eventual re-recording. The album represents an important chapter in Swift's career, showing her ability to reinvent herself and create art from personal adversity. Many fans consider it a concept album that tells a complete story of falling in love while navigating public perception and personal growth.
