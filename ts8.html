Folklore

Eighth Studio Album • 2020

Indie Folk Grammy Winner 17 Songs

A surprise album written and recorded during quarantine, marking Swift's venture into indie folk and alternative rock.

About the Album

Folklore is Taylor Swift's eighth studio album, surprise-released in July 2020 during the COVID-19 pandemic. The album marks a dramatic departure from her previous pop work, embracing indie folk, alternative rock, and chamber pop sounds. Created in isolation with collaborators including Aaron Dessner of The National and Jack Antonoff, Folklore presents a collection of stories and character studies rather than autobiographical material. Swift described it as "a collection of songs and stories that flowed like a stream of consciousness" during an unprecedented time.

The album features critically acclaimed tracks including "cardigan," "exile" featuring Bon Iver, "the 1," and "august." "cardigan" debuted at number one on the Billboard Hot 100, making Swift the first artist to debut a song and music video simultaneously at number one. The album's storytelling weaves together fictional narratives, historical references, and personal reflections, creating what many consider to be Swift's most mature and sophisticated work to date.

Folklore was both a critical and commercial success, debuting at number one on the Billboard 200 and breaking the record for the biggest first-day album streams by a female artist on Spotify. The album won Album of the Year at the 63rd Grammy Awards in 2021, making Swift the first female artist to win this award three times. It also received widespread critical acclaim, with many publications naming it among the best albums of 2020.

The album's release was accompanied by a series of limited edition physical releases and merchandise, creating a collectible experience for fans during lockdown. Folklore represents a significant artistic evolution for Swift, demonstrating her versatility as a songwriter and willingness to explore new genres. The album's success proved that surprise releases and artistic reinvention could thrive even in challenging circumstances.
