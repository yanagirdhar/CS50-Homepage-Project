The sister album to Folklore, continuing the indie folk exploration with autumnal themes and richer storytelling.

About the Album

Evermore is Taylor Swift's ninth studio album, surprise-released in December 2020 as a companion piece to Folklore. Described by Swift as "Folklore's sister record," Evermore continues the indie folk and alternative rock exploration but with a more autumnal, wintry atmosphere. The album features many of the same collaborators as Folklore, including Aaron Dessner and Jack Antonoff, and introduces new collaborators like HAIM and The National. Swift called it "another collection of songs we couldn't stop writing" during the creative burst that began with Folklore.

The album includes standout tracks like "willow," "champagne problems," "no body, no crime" featuring HAIM, and "evermore" featuring Bon Iver. "willow" debuted at number one on the Billboard Hot 100, making Swift the first artist to debut two songs and music videos at number one in the same year. The album's storytelling expands on Folklore's narrative approach, with songs exploring murder mysteries, small-town dramas, and complex emotional landscapes. Many tracks feature more prominent piano and string arrangements, giving the album a richer, more orchestral sound than its predecessor.

Evermore debuted at number one on the Billboard 200, making Swift the first artist to debut two albums at number one in the same calendar year since 2015. The album received widespread critical acclaim, with many critics praising its lyrical sophistication and musical cohesion. While not as commercially massive as some of Swift's previous work, Evermore developed a dedicated following among fans and critics who appreciated its artistic ambition and autumnal atmosphere.

The album represents the conclusion of what Swift called the "folklorian woods" chapter of her career. Released during the holiday season, its warm, wintry sound and themes of reflection and closure resonated with listeners navigating the second year of the pandemic. Evermore demonstrates Swift's continued growth as a storyteller and her ability to sustain creative momentum across multiple projects, solidifying her reputation as one of her generation's most prolific and versatile songwriters.
