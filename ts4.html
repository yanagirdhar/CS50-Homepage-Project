Red

Fourth Studio Album • 2012

Pop Rock Country 30 Songs

The transitional album where Taylor Swift explored heartbreak through a kaleidoscope of genres and emotions.

About the Album

Red is Taylor Swift's fourth studio album, released in 2012 when she was 22 years old. The album represents a significant musical transition, moving further away from her country roots and experimenting with pop, rock, and electronic elements. The title "Red" symbolizes the intense, tumultuous emotions explored throughout the album—particularly those associated with love and heartbreak. Swift described the album as being about "the tumultuous, crazy, insane, intense, semi-toxic relationships that I've experienced in the last two years."

The album features some of Swift's most iconic and critically acclaimed songs, including "We Are Never Ever Getting Back Together," "I Knew You Were Trouble," "22," and the 10-minute version of "All Too Well." These tracks showcase Swift's evolution as a songwriter, tackling more complex emotions and incorporating diverse musical styles. "All Too Well" in particular has become a fan favorite and is widely regarded as one of her best songwriting achievements, telling a detailed story of a past relationship's rise and fall.

Red was both a commercial and critical success, debuting at number one on the Billboard 200 and selling 1.21 million copies in its first week. It became the fastest-selling album in over a decade at the time. The album received a Grammy nomination for Album of the Year in 2014, and "I Knew You Were Trouble" earned a nomination for Best Pop Solo Performance. The album's success marked Swift's full transition from country star to global pop phenomenon.

In 2021, Swift released "Red (Taylor's Version)," which includes re-recordings of all original tracks plus 9 new "From the Vault" songs. The re-release gained significant attention for including the highly anticipated 10-minute version of "All Too Well," complete with a short film starring Sadie Sink and Dylan O'Brien. The album broke multiple streaming records and demonstrated Swift's continued relevance and connection with her fanbase. The 10-minute "All Too Well" even reached number one on the Billboard Hot 100, making it the longest song ever to top the chart.
