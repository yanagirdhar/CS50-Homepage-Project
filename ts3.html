Speak Now

Third Studio Album • 2010

Country Rock Solo Written 22 Songs

Taylor Swift's entirely self-written album, showcasing her growth as a songwriter and storyteller.

About the Album

Speak Now is Taylor Swift's third studio album, released in 2010 when she was 20 years old. The album is particularly significant because Swift wrote every song entirely by herself, with no co-writers. This was her response to critics who questioned her songwriting abilities, proving she could craft an entire album solo. The album blends country, pop, and rock influences.

The album features some of Swift's most personal and storytelling-focused songs, including "Mine," "Back to December," "Mean," and "Dear John." Each song is said to be inspired by real people and events from her life, with Swift using the album as a platform to "speak now" about things she wished she had said in the moment. The confessional nature of the lyrics resonated deeply with fans and critics alike.

Speak Now was a commercial success, debuting at number one on the Billboard 200 and selling over one million copies in its first week. It produced several hit singles including "Mine," "Back to December," and "Mean." The latter won two Grammy Awards in 2012 for Best Country Song and Best Country Solo Performance, further establishing Swift's credibility as a songwriter.

In 2023, Swift released "Speak Now (Taylor's Version)" as part of her project to re-record her first six studio albums. The re-recorded version includes all original tracks plus 6 new "From the Vault" songs that were written during the Speak Now era but not included on the original album. The release was celebrated by fans and marked another successful chapter in Swift's quest to own her masters.
