1989

Fifth Studio Album • 2014

Synth-Pop Grammy Winner 22 Songs

Taylor Swift's official transition to pop music, inspired by 1980s synth-pop and named after her birth year.

About the Album

1989 is Taylor Swift's fifth studio album, released in 2014 when she was 24 years old. The album marks her official transition from country to pop music, inspired by the synth-pop sounds of the 1980s. Named after Swift's birth year, 1989 represents a reinvention of her musical identity and a declaration of independence from her country music roots. Swift described the album as her "first documented, official pop album."

The album features some of Swift's biggest commercial hits, including "Shake It Off," "Blank Space," "Style," and "Bad Blood." These songs dominated charts worldwide and became cultural phenomena. "Shake It Off" in particular became an anthem for resilience and self-acceptance, while "Blank Space" cleverly satirized media portrayals of Swift's dating life. The album's production, characterized by synthesizers, drum machines, and electronic elements, represented a dramatic departure from her previous work.

1989 was both a critical and commercial triumph, debuting at number one on the Billboard 200 and selling 1.287 million copies in its first week—the highest first-week sales of any album since 2002. The album won Album of the Year at the 58th Grammy Awards in 2016, making Swift the first woman to win this award twice for solo albums. It also won Best Pop Vocal Album, and "Bad Blood" won Best Music Video. The album spent 11 weeks at number one on the Billboard 200 and was certified 9× Platinum in the US.

In 2023, Swift released "1989 (Taylor's Version)," completing her re-recording project for this era. The re-release includes all original tracks plus 5 new "From the Vault" songs. The album broke multiple streaming records upon release, becoming the most-streamed album in a single day on Spotify. The re-recorded version maintains the synth-pop spirit of the original while offering fans new material from the 1989 era, including the fan-favorite track "'Slut!'" which generated significant buzz for its provocative title and themes.
