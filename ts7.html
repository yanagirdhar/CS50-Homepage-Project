Lover

Seventh Studio Album • 2019

Pop Synth-Pop 18 Songs

A romantic celebration of love in all its forms, marking a return to brighter, more colorful pop after Reputation.

About the Album

Lover is Taylor Swift's seventh studio album, released in 2019. Following the darker tones of Reputation, Lover represents a return to brighter, more optimistic pop music. The album explores love in its many forms—romantic, platonic, and self-love—with a pastel-colored aesthetic that contrasts sharply with its predecessor. Swift described the album as "a love letter to love itself," featuring some of her most joyful and vulnerable songwriting.

The album features hit singles including "Me!" featuring Brendon Urie, "You Need to Calm Down," "Lover," and "The Man." "You Need to Calm Down" became particularly notable for its advocacy for LGBTQ+ rights and its star-studded music video featuring many prominent LGBTQ+ celebrities. The title track "Lover" has become a modern wedding standard, praised for its romantic, timeless quality.

Lover debuted at number one on the Billboard 200, selling 867,000 copies in its first week. It was the best-selling studio album of 2019 in the United States. The album received generally positive reviews from critics, who praised its songwriting and thematic coherence. Lover is also notable for being Swift's first album owned entirely by her from the beginning, giving her full control over her masters.

Unlike her earlier albums, Lover doesn't require a "Taylor's Version" re-recording since Swift owns the masters. The album represents a period of personal happiness and creative freedom for Swift, showcasing her ability to bounce back from adversity with optimism and joy. The Lover era was cut short by the COVID-19 pandemic, preventing a full tour, but the album remains a fan favorite for its uplifting messages and catchy melodies.
