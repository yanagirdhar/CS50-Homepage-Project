Fearless

Second Studio Album • 2008

Country Pop Grammy Winner 27 Songs

Taylor Swift's breakthrough album that won Album of the Year at the Grammys and defined a generation.

About the Album

Fearless is Taylor Swift's second studio album, released in 2008 when she was 18 years old. The album marked a significant evolution in her sound, blending country with pop elements and establishing her as a mainstream superstar. It became the best-selling album of 2009 in the United States.

The album features some of Swift's most iconic hits including "Love Story," "You Belong with Me," and "White Horse." These songs captured the hearts of millions with their relatable lyrics about teenage romance, heartbreak, and fairy-tale aspirations. "Love Story" in particular became a cultural phenomenon, reimagining Romeo and Juliet with a happy ending.

Fearless made history at the 52nd Grammy Awards, where it won Album of the Year, making Taylor Swift (at age 20) the youngest artist to ever win this prestigious award at that time. The album also won Best Country Album, while "White Horse" won Best Female Country Vocal Performance and Best Country Song. This recognition solidified Swift's position as a major force in the music industry.

In 2021, Swift re-recorded and released "Fearless (Taylor's Version)" as the first of her re-recorded albums, following the sale of her original masters. The new version includes all original tracks plus 6 new "From the Vault" songs, giving fans new material while allowing Swift to regain control of her early work. The re-release was both a commercial success and a powerful statement about artists' rights.
