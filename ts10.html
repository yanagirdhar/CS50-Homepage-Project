Tenth Studio Album • 2022

A concept album exploring 13 sleepless nights throughout Swift's life, blending pop production with intimate storytelling.

About the Album

Midnights is Taylor Swift's tenth studio album, released in October 2022. Described as "the stories of 13 sleepless nights scattered throughout my life," the album is a concept work that returns to pop music while incorporating elements of dream pop, chill-out, and electronica. Working primarily with longtime collaborator Jack Antonoff, Swift created an album that feels both intimate and atmospheric, exploring themes of self-reflection, anxiety, love, and vengeance during the quiet hours of the night.

The album features hit singles "Anti-Hero," "Lavender Haze," "Midnight Rain," and "Karma." "Anti-Hero" became one of Swift's most successful singles, breaking multiple streaming records and spending eight weeks at number one on the Billboard Hot 100. The song's confessional lyrics about self-doubt and insecurity resonated widely with listeners. Three hours after the initial release, Swift surprise-released the "3am Edition" with seven additional tracks, expanding the album's exploration of nocturnal themes.

Midnights broke numerous records upon release, becoming the most-streamed album in a single day on Spotify (with over 185 million streams) and the first album in history to claim the entire top 10 of the Billboard Hot 100 simultaneously. The album debuted at number one on the Billboard 200, selling 1.578 million copies in its first week—the largest sales week for any album since 2015. Midnights won Album of the Year at the 2024 Grammy Awards, making Swift the first artist to win this award four times.

The Midnights era was accompanied by the massively successful Eras Tour, which became the highest-grossing concert tour of all time. The album represents a synthesis of Swift's musical evolution, blending the pop sensibilities of 1989 with the intimate storytelling of Folklore and Evermore. Its commercial and critical success solidified Swift's status as one of the most influential artists of her generation, capable of breaking records more than 15 years into her career.
